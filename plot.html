A Symphony of Dreams and Reality

While stuck in Los Angeles traffic ("Another Day of Sun"), Sebastian "Seb" Wilder, a jazz pianist, has a moment of road rage directed at aspiring actress Mia Dolan. After a hard day at work, Mia's subsequent audition goes awry when the casting director takes a phone call during an emotional scene. That night, her roommates take her to a lavish party in the Hollywood Hills, where she hopes for a breakthrough to jump-start her career ("Someone in the Crowd"). After her car is towed, she walks home in disappointment.

During a gig at a restaurant, Seb slips into a passionate jazz improvisation, despite the owner's warning to only play traditional Christmas pieces. Mia overhears him playing as she passes by ("Mia and Sebastian's Theme"). Moved, she enters the restaurant and observes Seb being fired for his disobedience. As he storms out, Mia attempts to compliment him, but he brushes her off.

Months later, she runs into Seb at a party where he plays in a 1980s pop cover band. she teases him by requesting "I Ran (So Far Away)", a song he considers an insult for "a serious musician". After the gig, they walk to their cars and lament wasting a lovely night together despite their clear chemistry ("A Lovely Night").

The next day, Seb arrives at Mia's workplace, and she shows him around the Warner Bros movielot, where she works as a barista, while expressing her passion for acting. Seb takes her to a jazz club, describing his passion for jazz and his desire to open his own club. They warm to each other ("City of Stars"). Seb invites Mia to a screening of Rebel Without a Cause and she accepts, forgetting a date with her boyfriend Greg. Bored with the double date, she goes to the theater and finds Seb as the film begins. When the screening is interrupted due to a projector malfunction, Seb and Mia spend the rest of the evening together with a romantic visit to the Griffith Observatory ('Planetarium').

After more failed auditions, Mia decides, with Seb's encouragement, to write a one-woman play. He begins to perform regularly at a jazz club ("Summer Montage"), and the two move in together. Seb's former classmate Keith invites him to be the keyboardist in his new jazz fusion band, which will give him a steady income. Although dismayed by the band's pop style, Seb signs on after overhearinging Mia trying to convince her mother that he is working on his career. The band finds success, but when Mia attends one of their concerts ("Start a Fire") she is disturbed, knowing Sebastian does not enjoy their music.

During the band's first tour, Seb and Mia have an argument; she accuses him of abandoning his dreams, while he claims that she liked him more when he was unsuccessful because it made her look better. Mia leaves, insulted and frustrated. Two weeks later, Seb misses Mia's play due to a photoshoot he had forgotten about. The play is a disaster; few people attend, and Mia overhears dismissive comments about her performance. Unable to forgive Seb for missing her play and their previous argument, Mia breaks up with Seb and moves back to her hometown of Boulder City, Nevada, unable to pay the theater back.

Seb receives a phone call from a prominent casting director who attended Mia's play and invites her to audition for an upcoming film. He drives to Boulder City and persuades her to attend which she reluctantly does. During the audition, Mia is asked to tell a story. In response, she sings about how her aunt, a former stage actress who eventually died from alcoholism, inspired her to chase her dreams ("Audition(The Fools Who Dream)"). Seb, confident the audition was a success, encourages her to devote herself to the opportunity in Paris. The two profess they will always love each other but are uncertain of their future.

Five years later, Mia is a famous actress and married to a different man, with whom she has a daughter. One night, the couple stumble upon a jazz bar. Recognizing the "Seb's" logo she had once designed, Mia realizes that Seb has opened his own jazz club. When he notices Mia in the crowd, Seb begins to play their love theme on the piano. A dream sequence unfolds in which the two imagine what might have been had their relationship thrived ("Epilogue") along with their careers. Seb and Mia acknowledge each other with a silent exchange of smiles before she leaves, each going their own separate ways.

The director

Damien Chazelle, the visionary director behind the cinematic gem La La Land, is a filmmaker known for his distinct style, unwavering passion for storytelling, and a penchant for exploring the complexities of ambition and artistic pursuits.

Born on January 19, 1985, in Providence, Rhode Island, Chazelle demonstrated an early affinity for the arts. He delved into filmmaking during his time at Harvard University, where he directed his first feature film, Guy and Madeline on a Park Bench (2009), a jazz-infused musical that showcased his unique approach to storytelling.

Chazelle's breakthrough, however, came with Whiplash (2014), a tense and riveting drama centered around the intense relationship between a jazz drummer and his demanding instructor. The film garnered widespread acclaim, earning three Academy Awards, including Best Supporting Actor for J.K. Simmons and a nomination for Best Picture. Whiplash showcased Chazelle's ability to create compelling narratives that explore the pursuit of excellence and the sacrifices one makes in the relentless pursuit of artistic perfection.

Following the success of Whiplash, Chazelle tackled the ambitious project of La La Land (2016), a musical that paid homage to the golden age of Hollywood while infusing a contemporary sensibility. The film not only earned critical acclaim but also made history at the 89th Academy Awards, where Chazelle became the youngest person ever to win the Academy Award for Best Director.

Chazelle's directorial style is characterized by his love for immersive storytelling, dynamic camera work, and a deep appreciation for music as a narrative tool. His films often explore the intersection of passion and obsession, with a keen focus on the sacrifices individuals make to achieve their dreams.

Beyond his work in film, Chazelle's foray into television with the Netflix series The Eddy continued to showcase his commitment to exploring the transformative power of music within storytelling.

With a relatively short but impactful filmography, Damien Chazelle has established himself as a director with a distinctive voice in the industry. His ability to blend traditional storytelling with innovative cinematic techniques has earned him a place among the most promising and influential directors of his generation. As audiences eagerly anticipate his future projects, Chazelle's name is sure to remain synonymous with cinematic excellence and a deep understanding of the human spirit.
